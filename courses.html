Course Information

Available Courses:

Bricklaying

We offer a variety of Bricklaying courses In this department. Whether you’re new to the trade or looking to develop your expertise, our practical training covers everything from basic blockwork and bricklaying techniques to more advanced skills in structural and decorative brickwork.

You’ll gain hands-on experience in measuring, setting out, laying bricks and blocks, and finishing all within a supportive, industry-standard workshop environment.

These courses open the door to a range of careers in construction, including work as a site bricklayer, craft mason, or opportunities to progress into site supervision, construction management, or self-employment.

Entry Requirements:

GCSE grades G and above (or equivalent) in English or Welsh (first language) and maths or WJEC L1/2 Constructing the Built Environment or mature student.

Students will be required to purchase basic Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), including safety boots and workwear, before starting practical workshop sessions.

Applicants should also have an interest in the chosen craft and evidence of practical ability would be an advantage.

Mature learners without formal qualifications are encouraged to apply and will be considered based on experience and interview performance.
